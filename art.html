Biography

Maryanne Peters is a mostly self-taught artist from Toronto, Canada, creating visionary art. She has taken a couple of workshops with artists she admires: Alex Grey and Chris Dyer. She plans to do more such workshops in the future, with different artists, believing that each has something different to teach. Some of the artists she has been inspired by include Etien, Olga Klimova, Neil Gibson, Bosch, and Tom LaPierre

She works with acrylic paint, and has recently added spraypaint and airbrush in order to challenge herself and learn fresh skills and techniques. She hopes to produce a lot of street art in warmer months, as well as continue painting canvas and the odd skateboard

Artist Statement

I paint and draw predominantly for meditation and healing. I find when I’m making art that my breathing naturally slows and deepens, and I’m able to relax and free my mind from the endless looping thoughts that create stress. I hate to use the cliche that I am ‘manifesting creation’, but I really do feel like I am pulling from an endless pool of creation to see what comes out - fishing for novelty and diversity.

I am inspired by natural and imagined life forms, as well as the concept of sacred geometry as the basic pattern from which all life emerges. I can begin with the same basic pattern, and have seemingly infinite possible directions. While I begin with a plan in mind, small choices along the way create an outcome that often surprises even me.

My art is intended to illicit a sense of harmonious interdependencies between all the various elements, all leading to a common center or origin. I don’t want any of it to seem too serious, but rather characterized as friendly, inviting, even whimsical.

I have recently started working with spray paint and airbrush - I welcome the learning curve for these new media in hopes that it can help break down existing barriers and give my art new depth and subtlety. Follow me on Instagram (@macpetersart) to see my progress on this exciting new journey!

Check out my urban sketches as well - I plan on doing a lot more of these.
