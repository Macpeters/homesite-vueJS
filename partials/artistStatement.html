Maryanne Peters

Artist Statement

  • I paint and draw predominantly for meditation and healing. I find when I’m making art that my breathing naturally slows and deepens, and I’m able to relax and free my mind from the endless looping thoughts that create stress. I hate to use the cliche that I am ‘manifesting creation’, but I really do feel like I am pulling from an endless pool of creation to see what comes out - fishing for novelty and diversity.
  • I am inspired by natural and imagined life forms, as well as the concept of sacred geometry as the basic pattern from which all life emerges. I can begin with the same basic pattern, and have seemingly infinite possible directions. While I begin with a plan in mind, small choices along the way create an outcome that often surprises even me.
  • My art is intended to illicit a sense of harmonious interdependencies between all the various elements, all leading to a common center or origin. I don’t want any of it to seem too serious, but rather characterized as friendly, inviting, even whimsical
  • I paint with acrylic, uv reactive/glow-in-the-dark, generally on raw, unprimed, unstretched canvas which hangs like tapestry, lightweight and unattached. My latest project leans more toward 3d spaces, rather than just 2d images. Rather than starting with a quadrilateral, I’ve started with a simple triangle, which I can paint front and back, and in duplicate. With these I’ll have more flexible, modular pieces which can transform into configurations.
